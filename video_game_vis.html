Background

A video game is an electronic game that involves a user interacting with a user interface or device to create audio and visual feedback from an electronic device. The earliest known video game was from 1947, a "cathode-ray tube amusement device" by Thomas T. Goldsmith Jr. and Estle Ray Mann, that allows a user to use an analog device to control a dot to simulate a missle firing at targets. From then on, more inventions with the goal of playing a game were introduced, giving way to the video game industry. Back then, video games could only be played on an arcade machine (How many people still remember those?!) or consoles dedicated to games, which then evolved to games that can be played on a computer or mobile device, with enhanced graphics, sound, and many more features. When video games were starting out, many people, mostly adults and parents, would argue that video games were nothing but mere time-wasters and would not bring any benefits. Now, playing and developing video games have become something more than just a hobby, it is a business, with its own market and industry. And with the research in technological fields such as virtual reality and augmented reality, its growth only continues. This website aims to visualise and discuss some relevant topics relating to video games with the target audience being those that have reservations regarding video games as a whole.

Video games aren't excluded to only computers, they can be played on a variety of devices. The simplest way to define the type of video game is the of device that they can be played on. These can be classified into 3 categories: Computers (PC), Console (PS5, XBOX), and Mobile Phones. The chart above shows the total revenue made by video games based on device type in the year 2022. Mobile games managed to make a total of $103.5 billion, more than PC and Console games combined. As phones nowadays are powerful enough to be a personal home computer that can be carried anywhere, games end up becoming more accessible to play.

This chart shows the types of business models used for video game monetization and their popularity among game developers. A survey was used to collect the data on which business models were used by game developers for their games. The survey allows for multiple models to be chosen by the respondents, hence the percentage values adding up to more than 100%. This is mainly because games usually use more than one business model in their games rather than just the one. One example is the Call of Duty franchise where not only do the games use a digital premium model, they also have paid in-game items as well. When it comes to digital premium games, which is selling a digital game, it is the most popular among developers, followed by free-to-download games.

Free-To-Play or Free-To-Download (F2P) games are games that do not require the user to pay any money to obtain or play the game. However, if that is the case, it can be easily assumed that these tpyes of games do not make any money for the developers. Based on this chart, this is not the case as it shows the worldwide revenue of games that are F2P from 2018 to 2024. This is because games will typically use another business model along with the F2P model to generate income. For example, Team Fortress 2 is a F2P computer game that contains items, usually hats, in the game that can be bought by the player using real money to change their characters' appearance. The reason for F2P console games having very low revenue compared to PC and mobile is that most console games adopt a digital premium model.
