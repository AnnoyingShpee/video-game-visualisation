Introduction

A video game is an electronic game that involves a user interacting with a user interface or device to create audio and visual feedback from an electronic device. The earliest known video game was from 1947, a "cathode-ray tube amusement device" by Thomas T. Goldsmith Jr. and Estle Ray Mann, that allows a user to use an analog device to control a dot to simulate a missle firing at targets. From then on, more inventions with the goal of playing a game were introduced, giving way to the video game industry. Back then, video games could only be played on an arcade machine (How many people still remember those?!) or consoles dedicated to games, which then evolved to games that can be played on a computer or mobile device, with enhanced graphics, sound, and many more features. When video games were starting out, many people, mostly adults and parents, would argue that video games were nothing but mere time-wasters and would not bring any benefits. Now, playing and developing video games have become something more than just a hobby, it is a business, with its own market and industry. And with the research in technological fields such as virtual reality and augmented reality, its growth only continues. This website aims to visualise and discuss some relevant topics relating to video games with the target audience being those that have reservations regarding video games as a whole in the UK or those who wish to know more regarding it.

Games as a Business

With how much revenue the video game industry generates, the question now is: "How does the video game industry make money?". It does not simply earn money just by selling games as this section will show. Just like any other industry, the video game market is a business, with different models that allow companies and developers to generate revenue with their games.

This chart shows the types of business models used by developers to monetize their video games and their popularity among them. A survey was used to collect the data on which business models were used by game developers for their games. The survey allows for multiple models to be chosen by the respondents, hence the percentage values adding up to more than 100%. This is mainly because games usually use more than one business model in their games rather than just one. One example is the Call of Duty franchise where not only do the games use a digital premium model, they also have paid Downloadable Content (DLC) not included in the standard game (Source). Free-To-Play or Free-To-Download (F2P) games are games that do not require the user to pay any money to obtain or play the game. However, if that is the case, it can be easily assumed that these tyes of games do not make any money for the developers. Based on this chart, this is not the case as it shows the worldwide revenue of games that are F2P from 2018 to 2024. This is because games will typically use another business model along with the F2P model to generate income. For example, Team Fortress 2 is a F2P computer game that contains items, usually hats, in the game that can be bought by the player using real money to change their characters' appearance. Based on the chart, F2P mobile games generate the most revenue compared to PC and console games every year. The reason for F2P console games having very low revenue compared to PC and mobile is that most console games adopt a digital premium model.

Free-To-Play Example (Genshin Impact)

F2P games have become very common in the video game industry, appearing mostly in the mobile and PC areas. As players can easily download these games and play straight away without the need to pay, game companies have to use one or more business models to monetize the game, each with their upsides and downsides. The most common in many small F2P mobile games is the use of advertising of other games or products (Product placement) to generate ad revenue. The problem from this is that if the company uses too many, players can get frustrated if ads pop up too frequently which disrupts their gameplay. Another common method, typically seen Gacha games, is the use of paid in-game currencies. In-game currencies can be earned by playing the game, but players can pay money to buy more of the game's currency in order to progress faster in the game or get better items quicker. If the currency is difficult to obtain and players are required to pay, the game can be considered by players as non-F2P-friendly which can damage the game's reception. Hence, when developing F2P games, companies have to account for many factors to balance monetization and player satisfaction. This section visualises one example of a very successful F2P game called Genshin Impact, which is a free-to-play action role-playing game developed and published by miHoYo in China on the 28th September 2020.
